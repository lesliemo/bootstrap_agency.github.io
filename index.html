About

  • 2008-2015

    Humble Beginnings

    In exchange for my 4-year full tuition scholarship, I worked in integrated marketing & project administration. I helped attract donors and prospective students to Berea College to remain afloat after the 2008 downturn. After becoming an award receipient for several departmental grants, including the Peterson Spring Award in technology and applied design, I was offered a position with Berea Athletics to build and coordinate the information systems for their rec center and the CRM for their community wellness programs.

  • August 2015

    A High Tech Professional is Born

    Despite the fact that most of my web development experience had been limited to database query architecture & CMS platorms like Wordpress, I eventually acquired enough IT and technical experience to be entrusted to manage workflows for 7 web developers, 3 graphic designers, and 2 videographers... To be fair, the group was incredibly talented! My role was mostly to acquire clients and translate their project requirements into design briefs,, budgets and client contracts across the project lifecycle. In the meantime, I assisted with economic development programming for our LEAN startup event marketing efforts (e.g. 5across pitch competitions, Startup Weekend, Global Entrepreneurship Week). The Team Alpha internship program provided an incredible opportunity to not only learn about agile entrepreneurship, but to also connect with incredible mentors and learn how to build a rewarding, values driven, high performance culture.

  • August 2015

    Meet the Maker

    Rather than let my other skills atrophy, I used my digital marketing expertise to assist other community organizations including a tenure assisting Lexington's brand new local makerspace and the Urban Ninja project in their efforts to scale!

  • Jan 2017

    Digital Inclusion to-scale

    Had an unexpected career pivot when my instructional media position was eliminated after I relocated to Louisville! I still manage media for about 40 client accounts -- most of which are smaller tiered colleges. But I've discovered quite recently, that I've really missed being a part of a peer community & the civic engagement you get in the programming & design fields. Now that I've got a few more civic data & digital inclusion projects under my belt, it looks like I'll be back in the fray in no time!
